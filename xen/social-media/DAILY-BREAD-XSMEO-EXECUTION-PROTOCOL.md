# Daily Bread — XSMEO Execution Protocol

Status: Canonical / Active
Effective date: 2026-07-18
Owner: Xen Social Media Executive Office
Applies to: Daily Bread morning report, founder workflow, Xen Globe status view, Xen Vault, XVE review

## Purpose

This protocol converts the Xen Social Media Beginning Plan into a persistent Daily Bread operating discipline. The goal is to ensure the founder story, build evidence, content pipeline, public rollout, and weekly learning loop remain visible and actionable every day.

## Daily Bread Required Section

Every Daily Bread report must include a section titled **Xen Social Media Build** with the following fields:

1. Current phase: Archive, Foundation, Soft Launch, Publishing, Community, Proof, Ecosystem, or Legacy.
2. Today's single highest-priority action.
3. Recording requirement: founder statement, build session, voice note, screenshot, architecture artifact, or reflection.
4. Vault status: captured, named, stored, reviewed, approved, or blocked.
5. Publishing status: not ready, draft, review, approved, scheduled, published, or measured.
6. Truth-state label for every public claim: Vision, Concept, Canonical Specification, Prototype, Build in Progress, Demonstration, Pilot, Validated Capability, or Production Capability.
7. Risk check: privacy, confidential information, unsupported claims, consent, security, or reputational risk.
8. Audience-value check: what the audience will learn, feel, understand, or be able to do.
9. XVE check: strategic value, lead value, reuse value, owned-audience value, cost, and measurable outcome.
10. End-of-day reflection reminder.

## Daily Minimum Standard

A compliant day requires at least one of the following:

- Record a 1–3 minute founder reflection.
- Capture one meaningful build artifact.
- Record one breakthrough, mistake, correction, or unanswered question.
- Organize and name one Vault item.
- Draft or improve one public content asset.
- Review one published item and capture what was learned.

## Seven-Day Beginning Sequence

### Day 1 — Founder Origin Statement
Record the 2–5 minute founder statement and store the untouched original.

### Day 2 — Xen Build Vault
Create and verify folders for Founder Journal, Screen Recordings, Voice Notes, Architecture, Sketches, Breakthroughs, Mistakes and Revisions, Social Media Content, Photos, Milestones, and Approved Public Assets.

### Day 3 — Current-State Record
Answer: What is Xen? Why am I building it? What problem can it solve? What do I understand? What remains unclear? What comes next?

### Day 4 — Build Capture
Record selected architecture, decision, correction, breakthrough, integration, or capability-creation moments.

### Day 5 — Founder Journal
Use the five-question reflection: What did we build? What did I learn? What surprised me? What remains unclear? What comes next?

### Day 6 — Public Story Definition
Maintain the opening narrative: a person without a traditional technical background is learning to build an ambitious operating system with AI, one conversation, system, and decision at a time.

### Day 7 — Weekly Review
Select the strongest founder moment, question, breakthrough, visual artifact, and emotional statement. Move only approved items into Approved Public Assets.

## Weeks 2–3 Foundation Checklist

- Secure and document platform ownership.
- Enable two-factor authentication and recovery controls.
- Complete profile, biography, profile image, header, and destination link.
- Establish minimum brand system.
- Prepare five repeatable formats: Founder Journal, The Build, Xen Explained Simply, The Question, and Weekly Evolution.

## Week 4 Soft Launch Checklist

- Prepare first nine Instagram posts.
- Produce first YouTube founder video.
- Produce first three short-form videos.
- Verify claims and evidence before publication.
- Confirm landing page or owned-audience capture path.

## Continuing Weekly Rhythm

- 1 YouTube video.
- 2 short-form videos.
- 2 Instagram posts or carousels.
- 3–5 Instagram Stories.
- 2 LinkedIn posts.
- 3–5 X updates.
- 1 Weekly Evolution summary.

This is a target rhythm, not permission to sacrifice truth, quality, privacy, or sustainability.

## Daily Bread Alert Conditions

Daily Bread must elevate an alert when any of the following occurs:

- No meaningful archive capture for three consecutive days.
- Founder journal missed for three consecutive meaningful build days.
- A public claim lacks a truth-state label or supporting evidence.
- Approved content remains unpublished beyond its useful window.
- Publishing volume exceeds review capacity.
- Sensitive or private information may be exposed.
- Content activity produces attention but no durable audience, learning, lead, or asset value.
- The weekly review has not been completed.

## Daily Closing Questions

- What did Xen become today that it was not yesterday?
- What did Darren learn today?
- What evidence was preserved?
- What can be responsibly shared?
- What is the next honest question?
